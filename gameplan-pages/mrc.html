The Moon. The moon has always been there. Through your good times, and through your bad times. But what has it changed? It just keeps on watching you try over and over again. And that's how it will be forever.

 The Mercy of the Moon is an exploration of nihilism and nostalgia through the scope of absurdism. "Made for Dancing" sets the stage for how the narrator got to where he is, and "Mornings" describes cycles that the narrator finds difficult to break. Both songs feel light despite their lyrics thanks to their peppy, upbeat instrumentals, which is why they set the stage perfectly for "The Mercy of the Moon", which does not hold back in its pure, mad hopelessness.
